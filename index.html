Every year, NetworkED hosts a short term fellowship program which orients and equips children with tools to explore networks in the real world through different pathways. The fellowship is rigorous, immersive and challenging, as children investigate biological, physical and social networks through a range of digital and physical experiences. And of course, we love to goof around as much as we can!

The design of the fellowship is done well in advance in collaboration with domain experts, researchers and educators to craft a meaningful and explorative experience grounded in impact. Data and observations from the fellowship program feed into long term research and pedagogy development centered around understanding the science of network thinking in children.

Network Science is the study of patterns of connections in the real world. It is an emerging science which provides a wiring diagram of any complex real world system be it society, spread of diseases or even the wiring of the brain. It unpacks the similarities, differences, relationships and connectivity amongst all parts within the networks revealing the mechanistic nature of the world we live in. Through networks, we understand the behaviour of real world dynamics of complex systems, enabling us to better identify, manage, predict the problems worth solving for social change.
