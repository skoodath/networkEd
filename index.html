About Us

We’re a community of passionate researchers, educators and mentors with a desire to introduce networks to children. The goal is to create a thriving ecosystem to share best practices on methods and tools to help children master the art of generating insights using networks.

Who can join?
Anyone interested in how to enhance cognitive functions of children using network representations from real world experiences.

The
Fellowship

Every year, NetworkED hosts a short term fellowship program to orient and equip children to explore networks in the real world through various pathways.

learn more

The fellowship acts as a launchpad to explore real world complexity. The fellowship is immersive and challenging, as children investigate real world biological, physical and social networks through a range of digital and physical experiences. The design of the fellowship is done well in advance in collaboration with domain experts, researchers and educators to craft a meaningful and explorative experience grounded in impact. Data and observations from the fellowship program feed into long term research and pedagogy development centered around understanding the science of network thinking in children.

What is
network science?

Network Science is the study of patterns of connections in the real world

learn more

It is an emerging science which provides a wiring diagram of any complex real world system be it society, spread of diseases or even the wiring of the brain. It unpacks the similarities, differences, relationships and connectivity amongst all parts within the networks revealing the mechanistic nature of the world we live in.

Through networks, we understand the behaviour of real world dynamics of complex systems, enabling us to better identify, manage, predict the problems worth solving for social change.
